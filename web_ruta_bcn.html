Ruta de la Guerra Civil a Barcelona

Itinerari circular a peu · Aproximadament 2 hores · 10 punts clau de memòria històrica

Idioma / Language:

Descripció de la ruta

Aquesta ruta proposa un recorregut circular per alguns dels espais més significatius de Barcelona durant la Guerra Civil Espanyola (1936–1939). El punt d’inici i final és la Plaça Catalunya, i el trajecte passa pel Barri Gòtic, el centre històric, l’Eixample i el barri de Gràcia.

El recorregut està pensat per fer-se a peu, en aproximadament 2 hores, combinant caminades amb aturades d’uns 8–10 minuts a cada lloc per llegir la informació, observar l’espai i imaginar el context històric.

La ruta se centra en tres grans eixos: la vida quotidiana en temps de guerra, els espais de poder i control, i la memòria visible que encara resta a les façanes i places.

A cada punt tens un enllaç a un Kahoot de 3 preguntes perquè puguis jugar mentre ets físicament al lloc.

Punt d’inici i final Eix neuràlgic

Des d’aquí sortien columnes de milicians cap al front, s’hi celebraven mítings i era també un espai de propaganda i mobilització popular. Route description

This circular walking route explores some of the most significant sites in Barcelona related to the Spanish Civil War (1936–1939). It starts and ends at Plaça Catalunya and passes through the Gothic Quarter, the historic centre, the Eixample district and the Gràcia neighbourhood.

The route is designed to be done on foot in about 2 hours, combining walking sections with short stops to read the information, observe the spaces and imagine the historical context.

It focuses on everyday life in wartime, spaces of power and control, and the visible traces of the conflict that remain in the city.

Route points (pinpoints)

1. Plaça Catalunya – Heart of Republican Barcelona

Start & end point City hub

During the Civil War, Plaça Catalunya was a key political and social hub. Columns of volunteers departed from here to the front, political rallies were held in the square, and propaganda was widely distributed. Many surrounding buildings suffered shrapnel damage and aerial bombings.

Pinpoint: Plaça Catalunya, Barcelona

2. Portal de l’Àngel – Traces of crossfire

Shrapnel on façades Historic centre

Today, Portal de l’Àngel is a busy shopping street, but in 1936–1937 it was a scene of gunfire and clashes. Some façades still show bullet and shrapnel marks, a discreet yet powerful reminder of the fighting in the heart of the city.

Pinpoint: Portal de l’Àngel, Barcelona

Play Kahoot for this spot

3. Plaça de Sant Felip Neri – The tragedy of the bombings

Bombed in 1938 Victims’ memory

Plaça de Sant Felip Neri is one of the most moving places in Barcelona. In 1938 it was bombed, and dozens of people died, many of them children. The church façade still shows shrapnel scars around the entrance, preserved as a memorial to the victims.

Pinpoint: Plaça de Sant Felip Neri, Barcelona

Play Kahoot for this spot

4. Plaça del Rei – Archives and institutional power

Gothic Quarter Historical archives

Surrounded by medieval buildings, Plaça del Rei has long been associated with political and institutional power. During the war, some spaces were used to safeguard documents and archival material from destruction.

Pinpoint: Plaça del Rei, Barcelona

Play Kahoot for this spot

5. Via Laietana 10 – Police control and repression

Police building Political control

Via Laietana has been linked for decades with policing and repression. During the Civil War it already housed key police and administrative offices, and under Franco’s dictatorship it became notorious as a place of detention and interrogation.

Pinpoint: Via Laietana 10, Barcelona

Play Kahoot for this spot

6. Plaça d’Urquinaona – Barricades and internal conflict

May 1937 events Urban barricades

Plaça d’Urquinaona was one of the focal points of armed clashes during the May 1937 events, when different groups within the Republican side fought each other. It illustrates the internal divisions and tensions that also shaped the conflict.

Pinpoint: Plaça d’Urquinaona, Barcelona

7. Plaça del Diamant – Air-raid shelter and life under bombs

Air-raid shelter Gràcia district

Beneath Plaça del Diamant there is an air-raid shelter that protected civilians from the bombings. Barcelona was one of the first cities in history to be systematically bombed to terrorise the population in the rear.

Pinpoint: Plaça del Diamant, Barcelona

8. Casa de les Punxes – Modernist architecture used for surveillance

Modernism Strategic point

Casa de les Punxes is a striking modernist building. During the war, its height and position allowed good visual control over several streets in the Eixample district, showing how civilian architecture could acquire military or surveillance functions.

Pinpoint: Casa de les Punxes, Avinguda Diagonal 420, Barcelona

Main avenue Troop movements

This stretch of Avinguda Diagonal served as a major logistical axis. Military vehicles, ambulances and columns of fighters moved along this avenue, linking different parts of the city and the routes out towards the front.

Pinpoint: Junction of Avinguda Diagonal / Passeig de Sant Joan, Barcelona
